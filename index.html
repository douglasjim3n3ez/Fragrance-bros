Knowing different fragance families:

The last tip you have to keep in mind is that fragances come in different families for example orientals, that are mostly spice scents, like vainilla. Florals, that contain at least one type of flower. Gourmand, that refers to fragrances that smell like some kind of food, like bubble gum, toffee and caramel. And lastly citruses, that are fragrances that contain, grapefruit, lemon, lime and bergamot.

Oriental Spice Fragrances:

Oriental fragrances, are fragrances that have at least one type of spice in the the scent DNA, the most common types of oriental spices are cinnamon, vainilla, ultravani, pepper, pink pepper, coffee, tonka bean and oud. One example of a warm spicy fragrance is Tom Ford, Tobacco Vanille.

Floral Fragrances:

Floral fragrances, are fragrances that contain floral scents in their DNA, although floral is a scent mostly in womans perfumes, it is also present in some fragrances for men as well. An example of a floral perfume is Louis Vuitton, Les Sables Roses.

Citrus Fragrances:

Citrus fragrances, are fragrances that have some kind of citrus in their DNA and are fresh. Some type of cirtus notes are bergamot, orange, lemon, lime, blood orange and grapefruit. One example of a great citrus fragrance is Louis Vuitton, Imagination.

Gourmand and Sweet Fragrances:

Gourmand fragrances, are fragrances that often smell sweet or like a dessert or something you can eat. Some type of gourmand notes are sugar, bubble gum, caramel, butter and toffee. One example of a gourmand fragrance is Xerjoff, Italica.
